# Eurovision 2026 Grand Final — Voting Rules

## Countries & Voting Blocs

**35 countries** participate in the Grand Final. Each country votes, plus there is an additional "Rest of the World" televote bloc representing non-participating countries. All blocs contribute to the final scores.

## Two Independent Voting Streams

Every voting entity (each country + Rest of World) casts **two separate sets of points**:

- **Professional jury** — 7 members per country, must include at least 2 jurors aged 18–25. Eligible backgrounds include music journalists, critics, teachers, choreographers, stage directors, and experienced music industry figures. New for 2026: all jurors must sign a formal declaration confirming they will vote independently, not coordinate with other jurors before the contest, and not share their preferences online before the contest concludes.
- **Public televote** — via phone, SMS, or the official Eurovision app. Maximum 10 votes per payment method (reduced from 20 in previous years).

Neither juries nor public voters can vote for their own country's entry.

## Point Allocation

Each voting stream (jury and televote independently) awards points to their top 10 songs using the classic scale:

| Position | Points |
|----------|--------|
| 1st      | 12     |
| 2nd      | 10     |
| 3rd      | 8      |
| 4th      | 7      |
| 5th      | 6      |
| 6th      | 5      |
| 7th      | 4      |
| 8th      | 3      |
| 9th      | 2      |
| 10th     | 1      |

A single country can give a song a maximum of **24 combined points** — 12 from the jury and 12 from the televote.

## Final Score & Ranking

Jury points and televote points from all blocs are summed into a **single combined total**. The song with the highest total wins.

## Rest of the World Televote

The Rest of the World (RoW) vote was introduced in 2023 and continues in 2026. It allows viewers in countries not participating in the contest to have a say in the outcome.

**How it works:**

- Voting is conducted online via the official Eurovision app or website
- To accommodate global time zones, RoW voting opens **24 hours before the Grand Final** rather than during the live show
- Each vote costs €0.99 (converted to the voter's local currency)
- Voters can cast up to **10 votes** per payment method
- RoW voters cannot vote for entries from any country they are physically located in at the time of voting

**How RoW votes count:**

- The aggregate results from all RoW voters are compiled and their top 10 songs are identified
- Points are allocated using the same 12/10/8/7/6/5/4/3/2/1 scale
- These points count as if they were cast by **one additional participating country** — a single extra voting bloc added to the scoreboard alongside the 35 national blocs
- The RoW bloc is announced during the results show in the same way as national results

## Tiebreaker Rules

If two or more songs share an equal combined score, the following hierarchy applies:

1. **Most televote points** — the song with the higher total televote points wins
2. **Broadest televote support** — if still tied, the song that received televote points from the most individual countries wins
3. **Highest televote point values** — if still tied, compare 12-point scores, then 10-point scores, and so on down the scale
4. **Running order** — if all else is equal, the song that performed **earlier in the show** is declared the winner
